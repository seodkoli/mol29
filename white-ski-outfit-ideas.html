This layer should be made of a moisture-wicking material such as merino wool or synthetic fabrics like polyester. Avoid cotton as it absorbs sweat and can leave you feeling damp and chilled. The second layer, or insulating layer, should provide additional warmth while still allowing for breathability. Fleece jackets or vests are popular choices for this layer as they trap heat close to the body without adding bulk.

The outermost layer, also known as the shell layer, serves as protection against wind, snow, and rain.
